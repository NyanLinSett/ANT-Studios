Reading Tips

#1 Reading questions can be vague sometimes.

Questions may contain confusing answers- most of the time 2 possible answers- so make sure to read thoroughly and give time for reading tasks.

#2 You don't have to do all readings.

Most of the time, the teacher will cover it in the lecture if the material is from the textbook; but reading ahead will give you an advantage, so if you do have time to spare, make sure to read everything thoroughly.

#3 Read Widely

Read all sorts of writings- fiction, non-fiction, articles, blog posts, etc. If you read frequently, you will get used to reading different styles of writing and when you have to face reading questions in tests or in class, you'll be able to process the information a lot faster.

#4 Don't just read.

Take active notes and try to re-write what you have read in your own words, so that you can understand and retain the information much more efficiently.

“I would advise you to read with a pen in your hand and enter in a little book short hints of what you feel that is common or that may be useful; for this will be the best method of imprinting such portcullis in your memory.” -Benjamin Franklin
